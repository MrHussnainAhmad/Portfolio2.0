    The Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM) is a professional degree program that typically lasts four years and provides in-depth training in animal sciences1. The mission of the DVM program is to provide exceptional educational experiences, grounded in evidence-based, best practices in veterinary medical education, preparing competent, confident graduates to begin careers advancing the health of animals, people, and the environment2. The curriculum of a DVM program is designed to provide students with a broad foundation of knowledge and skills in comparative veterinary medicine. After gaining this foundation, students can choose a species-specific stream in small or large animal. The flexible design of the program allows students to focus on careers in small animal, large animal, equine, livestock, zoologic or mixed animal practice as well as poultry, laboratory animal, aquatic medicine, pathology, public health or research

    Who we are?

    A Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM) is a highly trained professional who is responsible for the prevention of disease and the diagnosis and treatment of health conditions in animals. This includes companion animals such as dogs and cats, livestock species like cattle and pigs, and exotic animals such as reptiles or birds.
    Veterinarians contribute to environmental conservation efforts. They often work with wildlife and play a key role in the study and preservation of endangered species
